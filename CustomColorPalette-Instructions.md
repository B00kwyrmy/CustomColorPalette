# Custom Color Palette Plugin
## Complete Guide: Installing and Using the Plugin

---

## Before You Begin

This guide will walk you through two things:

1. **Installing** the Custom Color Palette plugin on your Supernote device.
2. **Using** the plugin to apply custom colors to your handwriting, drawings, and shapes.

You will need:
- Your Supernote device (A5X, A5X2, Manta, or compatible model)
- A USB-C cable to connect your Supernote to your computer
- The plugin file — it is named **CustomColorPalette.snplg** and should have been provided to you by your plugin developer or downloaded from the project page.

---

## Part 1 — Installing the Plugin

### Step 1 — Connect your Supernote to your computer

Use your USB-C cable to plug your Supernote into your computer (Mac or Windows). Your Supernote screen will show a prompt asking how you want to use the connection.

### Step 2 — Choose File Transfer mode

On your Supernote screen, tap **"USB Storage"** or **"File Transfer"** (the exact wording may vary by firmware version). Your Supernote will now appear as a USB drive on your computer.

### Step 3 — Open the Supernote drive on your computer

- **On a Mac:** Look in the left sidebar of Finder for your Supernote. Click on it to open it.
- **On Windows:** Open File Explorer. Look under "This PC" for your Supernote device. Double-click to open it.

### Step 4 — Find the MyStyle folder

Inside the Supernote drive, you will see several folders. Look for one called **MyStyle** and open it.

### Step 5 — Copy the plugin file

Drag and drop the **CustomColorPalette.snplg** file into the **MyStyle** folder. You can also copy and paste it (Ctrl+C / Ctrl+V on Windows, or Command+C / Command+V on Mac).

Wait for the file to finish copying before moving on.

### Step 6 — Safely disconnect your Supernote

- **On a Mac:** Drag the Supernote drive icon to the Trash, or right-click it in Finder and choose "Eject."
- **On Windows:** Click the "Safely Remove Hardware" icon in the taskbar (bottom right), then choose your Supernote.

Unplug the USB cable.

### Step 7 — Open Settings on your Supernote

On your Supernote, tap the **Settings** icon. This is usually found by swiping down from the top of the screen or tapping the gear icon in the menu.

### Step 8 — Go to Apps, then Plugins

In Settings, tap **Apps**. Then tap **Plugins**. You should see a list of available plugins, including **Custom Color Palette**.

### Step 9 — Install the plugin

Tap **Custom Color Palette** and then tap **Install** (or **Enable**). The plugin will activate and a new button called **Colors** will appear in your note-taking toolbar.

**The plugin is now installed.** You are ready to use it.

## Part 2 — Using the Plugin

The Custom Color Palette plugin lets you change the color of handwriting, drawings, and shapes you have already written or drawn in your note. Think of it as a "paint bucket" for your notes.

> **A note about your screen:** Your Supernote uses an e-ink screen, which shows everything in shades of gray — similar to an e-reader or Kindle. The colors you apply are stored invisibly in your note file. They appear in full color when you export your note to PDF or open it on a phone, tablet, or computer. This is completely normal and expected.

### How to Recolor Something — Step by Step

#### Step 1 — Write or draw as you normally would

Use any of your Supernote pen tools to write words, draw shapes, or highlight text as you normally would. There is nothing special you need to do at this stage.

#### Step 2 — Use the Lasso tool to select what you want to recolor

The **Lasso tool** lets you circle and select content on your note page. It looks like a dotted oval icon in your toolbar.

1. Tap the **Lasso** tool to activate it.
2. Draw a circle or loop around the handwriting, drawing, or shape you want to recolor. Make sure your loop closes (the end of your line meets the beginning).
3. The selected content will be highlighted with a border around it, showing it is selected.

#### Step 3 — Open the Color Palette plugin

With your content still selected, tap the **Colors** button in the toolbar. The plugin panel will open, showing the color picker.

> **Tip:** If you already have something lasso-selected, you may also see a **Recolor** button in the lasso toolbar. Either button opens the same color picker.

#### Step 4 — Choose the tab for the type of content you are recoloring

At the top of the color picker, you will see four tabs:

- **Ink Pen** — for handwriting drawn with the standard ink pen
- **Needle Point** — for fine handwriting drawn with the needle point pen
- **Shapes** — for shapes, lines, and drawings
- **Highlighter** — for highlighted text or areas

Tap the tab that matches the type of content you selected.

#### Step 5 — Choose a color

You will see a list of colors. The color that is currently selected has a checkmark (✓) next to it.

Tap any color name in the list to select it. The checkmark will move to your chosen color.

If you do not tap any color, the plugin will use the **default**:
- Ink Pen, Needle Point, and Shapes default to **Black**
- Highlighter defaults to **Gray**

#### Step 6 — Apply the color

Tap the large button at the bottom of the screen. It will say something like:

> **Apply Black (#231F20) to Selection**

The plugin will change the color of everything you selected in Step 2.

#### Step 7 — Check your work (on a color screen)

Because your Supernote screen is grayscale, you will not see the color change on the device itself. To see the colors, export your note to PDF:

1. Tap the **Share** or **Export** icon on your note page.
2. Choose **Export as PDF**.
3. Open the PDF on your phone, tablet, or computer to see the colors in full.

#### Step 8 — Close the plugin when you are done

Tap the **Close** button in the top right corner of the color picker to return to your note.

---

## Tips and Tricks

- **You can recolor the same content multiple times.** Simply lasso-select it again and choose a different color.
- **Each tab remembers its last color.** If you picked Orange on the Ink Pen tab and then switched to Highlighter, the Ink Pen tab will still show Orange as selected when you come back.
- **You can select a mix of content types.** The lasso will grab everything inside it — strokes, shapes, and text boxes — and apply the same color to all of them.
- **Nothing is permanent until you leave the page.** If something looks wrong, you can undo using the standard Supernote undo button before navigating away.

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | What to do |
|---------|------------|
| I don't see the "Colors" button in my toolbar | Go to Settings → Apps → Plugins and make sure Custom Color Palette is installed and enabled. |
| The plugin button is grayed out or missing | Try closing and reopening your note, then check again. |
| The Apply button says "Applying…" for a long time | The plugin is working. Wait for it to finish — large selections with many elements may take a few seconds. |
| My content looks the same after applying | Remember, your Supernote screen is grayscale. Export to PDF to see the color. |
| The plugin shows an error about "No lasso selection" | You need to lasso-select your content *before* tapping Apply. Go back to Step 2 and select your content first. |
